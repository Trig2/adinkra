# Full Curriculum Outline
## Adinkra Digital Literacy & Heritage Platform

---

## Program Overview

The Adinkra Digital Literacy & Heritage Platform delivers comprehensive digital literacy education through Ghana's rich cultural framework of Adinkra symbols. Each module pairs a specific Adinkra symbol with essential digital skills, making learning contextually relevant and culturally resonant.

### Target Audience
- Market women and traders
- Senior citizens
- Rural community members
- Anyone seeking to improve digital literacy skills
- Age range: 18-70+

### Learning Format
- Mobile-first progressive web application
- Low-data consumption design
- Offline capability for areas with limited connectivity
- Available in English (with plans for Twi and Ga)
- Self-paced with optional community facilitation

## Module 1: **Gye Nyame** - Digital Safety & Privacy

**Symbol:** Gye Nyame (Except God)  
**Meaning:** Supremacy of God, divine protection  
**Digital Literacy Topic:** Cybersecurity & Data Privacy  
**Duration:** 45 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Beginner

### Cultural Connection
Just as Gye Nyame represents God's supreme protection over all things, we must also learn to protect our digital identity and personal information in the online world.

### Learning Objectives
By the end of this module, learners will be able to:
- Understand the importance of digital safety
- Create and manage strong passwords
- Recognize common online scams and fraud
- Protect their WhatsApp account
- Practice safe mobile money transactions

### Lesson Breakdown

#### Lesson 1.1: Introduction to Digital Safety (10 mins)
- **Content:**
  - Why digital safety matters in Ghana today
  - The digital threats we face
  - Basic principles of online protection
- **Activities:**
  - Real-life scenarios discussion
  - Identifying digital risks quiz

#### Lesson 1.2: Creating Strong Passwords (15 mins)
- **Content:**
  - Elements of a strong password
  - Password creation techniques
  - Password management best practices
  - When and how to change passwords
- **Activities:**
  - Password strength checker
  - Create your own strong password exercise

#### Lesson 1.3: Protecting Your WhatsApp (10 mins)
- **Content:**
  - WhatsApp security features
  - Two-step verification setup
  - Privacy settings configuration
  - Recognizing WhatsApp scams
- **Activities:**
  - Hands-on: Enable two-step verification
  - Scam identification exercise

#### Lesson 1.4: Mobile Money Safety (10 mins)
- **Content:**
  - Mobile money security rules
  - PIN protection best practices
  - Recognizing mobile money fraud
  - What to do if something goes wrong
- **Activities:**
  - Case study analysis
  - Safety checklist completion

### Assessment
- 10-question quiz (70% passing grade)
- Practical demonstration: Setting up WhatsApp 2FA
- Certificate of completion

### Resources Provided
- Printable safety checklist
- Emergency contact numbers
- Step-by-step guides with screenshots

## Module 2: **Sankofa** - Digital Communication & WhatsApp Mastery

**Symbol:** Sankofa (Return and Get It)  
**Meaning:** Learn from the past to build the future  
**Digital Literacy Topic:** Digital Communication  
**Duration:** 60 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Beginner

### Cultural Connection
Sankofa teaches us to learn from our past experiences. In digital communication, we build on traditional communication values—respect, clarity, and cultural sensitivity—while embracing new technologies.

### Learning Objectives
- Master WhatsApp for personal and business use
- Understand proper digital communication etiquette
- Use WhatsApp Business features effectively
- Manage group communications
- Share files, photos, and documents safely

### Lesson Breakdown

#### Lesson 2.1: WhatsApp Basics (15 mins)
- Setting up your profile
- Adding and managing contacts
- Sending messages, voice notes, and calls
- Status updates

#### Lesson 2.2: WhatsApp for Business (20 mins)
- WhatsApp Business vs. Regular WhatsApp
- Creating a business profile
- Using quick replies and automated messages
- Managing customer inquiries
- Building trust through digital communication

#### Lesson 2.3: Groups and Communities (15 mins)
- Creating and managing groups
- Group etiquette and best practices
- WhatsApp Communities for organizations
- Muting and managing notifications

#### Lesson 2.4: Advanced Features (10 mins)
- Sharing location
- Document sharing
- Voice and video calls
- Backup and restore

### Assessment
- Practical project: Create a business profile
- Group management simulation
- Communication etiquette quiz

## Module 3: **Duafe** - Mobile Money & Digital Finance

**Symbol:** Duafe (Wooden Comb)  
**Meaning:** Beauty, cleanliness, and feminine qualities  
**Digital Literacy Topic:** Digital Financial Literacy  
**Duration:** 50 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Beginner to Intermediate

### Cultural Connection
Just as Duafe represents care, attention to detail, and maintaining what is valuable, we must carefully manage our digital finances and maintain good financial records.

### Learning Objectives
- Master mobile money services (MTN, AirtelTigo, Telecel)
- Send and receive money safely
- Pay bills digitally
- Track transactions and manage budgets
- Avoid mobile money fraud

### Lesson Breakdown

#### Lesson 3.1: Mobile Money Fundamentals (15 mins)
- How mobile money works in Ghana
- Registering for mobile money
- Understanding fees and limits
- Checking your balance

#### Lesson 3.2: Transactions (15 mins)
- Sending money
- Receiving money
- Withdrawing cash
- Buying airtime and data

#### Lesson 3.3: Digital Payments (10 mins)
- Paying bills (ECG, water, DStv)
- Merchant payments
- Online shopping with mobile money

#### Lesson 3.4: Financial Safety & Record Keeping (10 mins)
- Avoiding mobile money fraud
- Keeping transaction records
- Budget tracking using mobile money
- Resolving disputes

### Assessment
- Transaction simulation exercise
- Fraud scenario identification
- Budget planning activity

## Module 4: **Adinkrahene** - Online Resources & Information Literacy

**Symbol:** Adinkrahene (Chief of Adinkra Symbols)  
**Meaning:** Leadership, excellence, charisma  
**Digital Literacy Topic:** Information Literacy & Online Resources  
**Duration:** 55 minutes  
**Difficulty:** Intermediate

### Cultural Connection
As Adinkrahene is the chief of all Adinkra symbols, it represents the ability to lead and make wise decisions. Online, we must lead ourselves wisely by finding reliable information and using digital resources effectively.

### Learning Objectives
- Navigate the internet effectively
- Identify reliable vs. unreliable information
- Use search engines efficiently
- Access government and health services online
- Understand fake news and misinformation

### Lesson Breakdown

#### Lesson 4.1: Internet Basics (15 mins)
- Understanding the internet
- Using web browsers
- Searching for information
- Bookmarking important sites

#### Lesson 4.2: Evaluating Online Information (15 mins)
- Fact vs. fiction online
- Identifying fake news
- Reliable sources in Ghana
- Critical thinking online

#### Lesson 4.3: Digital Government Services (15 mins)
- Ghana.gov.gh portal
- Ghana Card information
- Passport application online
- Other e-government services

#### Lesson 4.4: Online Learning & Development (10 mins)
- Free learning resources
- YouTube for skills development
- Educational apps
- Continuing your digital learning journey

### Assessment
- Information verification exercise
- Create a personal learning plan
- E-government service navigation task
