# Community Pilot Program Plan
## Adinkra Digital Literacy & Heritage Platform

---

## Executive Summary

The 4-week Community Pilot Program will test and refine the Adinkra Digital Literacy Platform with real users in authentic community settings. We will partner with two pilot communities in the Greater Accra region to deliver hands-on digital literacy training that respects and builds upon existing knowledge and cultural practices.

---

## Pilot Objectives

### Primary Objectives
1. **Validate Learning Approach:** Test the effectiveness of teaching digital literacy through Adinkra cultural framework
2. **Gather User Feedback:** Collect detailed feedback on content, platform usability, and learning outcomes
3. **Refine Curriculum:** Identify areas for improvement in content and delivery
4. **Test Technology:** Validate PWA performance on various devices and network conditions
5. **Build Community:** Create a foundation for word-of-mouth growth and community advocacy

### Success Metrics
- **Participation:** 80% attendance rate across 4 weeks
- **Completion:** 70% of participants complete at least one module
- **Satisfaction:** 85% satisfaction rating
- **Learning Outcomes:** 75% pass rate on module assessments
- **Technology:** Platform works on 95% of participant devices

## Pilot Communities

### Community 1: Makola Market Women's Association

**Location:** Makola Market, Central Accra  
**Partner:** Makola Market Women's Association  
**Participants:** 30 market women (ages 25-65)

**Why This Community:**
- High mobile phone ownership but varying digital literacy levels
- Active use of mobile money and WhatsApp for business
- Strong community organization and leadership
- Representative of Ghana's vibrant market trading community
- Potential for high impact on livelihoods

**Contact Person:** Auntie Grace Mensah, Association President  
**Venue:** Market Association Meeting Hall

### Community 2: Nungua Senior Citizens Center

**Location:** Nungua, Greater Accra  
**Partner:** Nungua Senior Citizens Welfare Center  
**Participants:** 25 senior citizens (ages 55-75)

**Why This Community:**
- Growing senior population interested in staying connected
- Need for safe digital communication with family
- Concerns about online fraud and security
- Represents underserved demographic in digital literacy
- Testimonials will resonate widely

**Contact Person:** Mr. Emmanuel Tetteh, Center Coordinator  
**Venue:** Community Center Main Hall

## Risk Management

### Potential Risks & Mitigation

#### Low Attendance
- **Risk:** Participants drop out due to competing priorities
- **Mitigation:** 
  - Flexible timing with community input
  - Attendance incentives (certificates, data bundles)
  - Strong community leader buy-in
  - Regular encouragement and follow-up

#### Technical Issues
- **Risk:** Platform doesn't work on old devices or poor networks
- **Mitigation:**
  - Pre-pilot device testing
  - Offline-first design
  - Backup devices available
  - Multiple connectivity options

#### Language Barriers
- **Risk:** English-only content limits understanding
- **Mitigation:**
  - Community liaisons speak local languages
  - Visual and practical demonstrations
  - Peer translation
  - Plan for Twi/Ga versions in future

#### Low Engagement
- **Risk:** Content doesn't resonate with participants
- **Mitigation:**
  - Culturally relevant examples
  - Immediate practical applications
  - Interactive and hands-on approach
  - Regular feedback and adaptation
